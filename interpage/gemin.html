Fire Opal Quality

Fire Opal exhibits beautiful, elegant, and shimmering color play effects, which makes the gemstone worth having. Fire Opal is desired and valued for its adularescence effect, luster, cut, clarity, rarity, and carat weight. Certain parameters determine the quality of Fire Opal and based on these parameters, the Fire Opal quality can be evaluated. The parameters include:

  • Origin

    – Mexico is, of course, the main source of the Mexican Fire Opal. Fire Opal is hugely mined in the Mexican states of Queretaro, Hidalgo, Guerrero, Michoacan, Julisio, Chihuahua, and San Luis Potosi. The most significant ones were found in Queretaro and are still very popular for producing the finest quality Fire Opal gemstone.

  • Color

    – Color is the most influential factor for evaluating the Fire Opals value. Pure white with fire is the most dominant hue although orange opal, black opal, green opal, blue, and Pink Opal also exist. A white Opal with fire is considered most desirable for its astrological purposes. Opal with dark body colors like black, blue, and green are equally valued as they display a perfect play-of-color above a dark background.

  • Cut

    : A properly and skillfully cut Fire Opal showcases brilliant luster and color while also hiding any visible flaws or inclusions. Such specimens are considered to be of a higher quality. A finely cut or faceted Fire Opal raw not only enhances the color and luster but also hides any visible flaws in the form of inclusions or surface errors.

  • Clarity

    : Fire Opal displays a transparent to translucent appearance. The best quality Fire Opal is free from eye-visible inclusions. Inclusions in Fire Opal mostly occur as fingerprints and thus it is necessary to find an eye-clean specimen to confirm its quality. An eye-clean Fire Opal with good color is generally considered to be of fine quality and hence sells for a higher price as compared to specimens with visible inclusions and surface flaws.

  • Carat Weight

    : Gem experts and Gem buyers prefer Fire Opal of larger size and weight. Fire Opal of larger size displays a high concentration, is more powerful, and shows maximum benefits. It is often advised to avoid purchasing gemstones with too high or too flat domes as they are more vulnerable to damage when customized in jewelry.

Cleaning and Care

Fire opal has a high water content which makes it sensitive to intense heat and exposure to strong light. It is an extremely fragile gemstone and sudden changes in humidity and temperature can affect the quality of the gemstone. However, with proper care, your most loved gemstone can last for a longer period. It is highly recommended to avoid cleaning this gemstone with lots of water. One can quickly get rid of all the dirt stuck in the gemstone by using a soft toothbrush and a mild dishwashing detergent. It is advisable to not dip the gemstone directly in water.
